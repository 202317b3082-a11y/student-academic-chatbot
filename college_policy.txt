Policy: If the OpenAthens activation link is missed or expires, the Library can resend it upon request from the registered email ID. However, due to a high volume of queries, there may be a slight delay in processing. Users are advised to activate their account promptly after receiving the email to avoid interruptions in accessing e-resources.
Access to various e-resources such as EBSCO Engineering Collection, JSTOR, ASME eJournal Collection, ACM Digital Library, McGraw Hill Access Engineering, Oxford University Press, IEEE Xplore, Springer Nature Link, and Taylor & Francis is available through OpenAthens authentication. Users need to log in to the OpenAthens portal using their institutional email ID, search for the desired platform, and access it directly with authenticated credentials. Similar procedures apply for accessing a variety of subscribed e-journals, databases, and platforms.
E-books from publishers like Taylor & Francis, PHI Learning, Packt Publishing, Cengage Learning, CBS Publisher, Cambridge Core, ProQuest, Wiley, and McGraw-Hill are also accessible via OpenAthens. After logging in, users can search for the platform, click on the relevant option, and access the available titles. Some platforms require users to click on “Continue” or “Borrow” options for reading, while optional login or registration features allow personalization.
OpenAthens acts as a Single Sign-On (SSO) system that enables seamless access to all library-subscribed resources. Users must activate their account within 24–48 hours of receiving the activation email, or at most within 90 days to avoid permanent deletion. Once activated, login credentials consist of the institutional email ID and the password set during activation. If the activation email is not found, users should check spam folders or contact the designated librarian.
All library e-resources must be accessed through OpenAthens, especially for remote usage. If access issues arise, reasons may include lack of subscription to a specific title, restricted coverage, or failure to use proper authentication. Users should ensure access via OpenAthens and provide detailed information such as resource name, platform, and error screenshots when reporting issues.
Pearson e-books require a separate account created by the Library upon request, even though OpenAthens authentication may still need to remain active during access. Users must log in through the Pearson portal using those credentials and can access books through their personal bookshelf. Password reset and account-related issues are managed through the Pearson portal or by contacting the Library.
The WILP Library resources listing and access are centralized within the OpenAthens portal, where users can search for platforms or directly view available subscriptions. Authentication ensures seamless navigation across publishers, and the availability of content strictly depends on institutional subscriptions rather than the entire publisher catalog.
Semester registration processes for WILP programs are conducted twice annually, with communication sent via official email and announcements posted on the eLearn portal. Students must complete fee payment and course selection on the ERP portal within deadlines. Newly admitted students are automatically registered during admission and do not need to register separately.
Students can manage academic planning using ERP tools such as performance sheets and course lists, ensuring they register only for pending or required courses. Course clashes, validation errors, or technical issues can be resolved by following specific guidelines, such as clearing cache, re-logging, or contacting support services.
Fee payment can be made through full payment options or EMI plans. Payment-related issues such as delays, failed transactions, duplicate deductions, or EMI processing timelines must be monitored carefully. Refunds for duplicate payments are processed within a few weeks, while unresolved issues can be addressed by contacting the accounts department.
Academic regulations specify limits on course registration, backlog handling, and eligibility for final semester dissertation or project work. Students must meet minimum CGPA requirements and clear all pending courses before registering for final components. Backlogs may extend program duration and require additional semester fees.
Examinations follow strict schedules with predefined slots, including regular and make-up exams. Students must attend required evaluation components such as assignments, mid-semester, and comprehensive exams. Missing both regular and make-up exams results in compulsory re-registration for that course.
At exam centers, students are required to follow guidelines such as reporting early, carrying valid ID, adhering to closed-book or open-book rules, and submitting answer scripts properly. Infrastructure is provided at centers, and students are not required to bring personal laptops.
Project work or dissertation is an individual effort aligned with the student’s program and organization. Students must select topics in consultation with supervisors and obtain necessary approvals before starting. Supervisors typically come from the student’s organization, although exceptions are allowed with justification and approval.
The project process involves multiple stages including abstract submission, mid-semester review, and final viva. Evaluation is conducted by BITS faculty, while supervisors guide the student. Reports must be submitted in PDF format, and plagiarism checks using tools like Turnitin are mandatory.
Confidentiality concerns with organizational data can be handled by using open-source datasets, simulated data, or adjusted project scopes. Students must ensure compliance with data policies and may need to provide no-objection certificates when working outside their organization.
Final semester grades for projects are non-letter grades such as Excellent, Good, Fair, Poor, or RRA, and they do not contribute to CGPA. Dissertation credits and evaluation guidelines are shared separately, and all submissions are done digitally without hard copy requirements.
After graduation, students receive transcripts, provisional certificates, and degree certificates based on approval processes. Certificates are dispatched to registered addresses, and any issues related to delays, undelivered documents, or pending dues must be resolved through the ERP system or support contacts.
Alumni services include certificate downloads, migration certificate applications, duplicate transcripts, and alumni membership registration. Digital certificates are also available through platforms like DigiLocker for eligible graduates, while physical documents are sent via post after successful completion of the program.
Convocation may be conducted either physically or virtually, with participation criteria defined by the institute. Students must adhere to registration procedures, dress codes, and communication guidelines. Virtual convocation may be conducted in immersive environments, and attending students must follow provided instructions.
Overall, academic processes, resource access, project guidelines, and post-graduation services are managed systematically through integrated platforms such as OpenAthens, ERP, and eLearn, ensuring structured support for students throughout their academic journey.
