It is computed as the weighted average of grade points obtained in all courses, where the weights correspond to the course units. Policy: About Work-Integrated Learning Programmes (WILP):
BITS Pilani is recognized as an Institution of Eminence under the UGC (Institution of Eminence Deemed to be Universities) Regulations, 2017. The Work-Integrated Learning Programmes (WILP) offered by BITS Pilani represent a distinctive set of educational offerings specifically designed for working professionals. These programmes extend the academic framework of courses delivered at BITS Pilani campuses and maintain parity with regular programmes in terms of academic rigour and unit or credit requirements. The programmes are structured to effectively integrate academic learning with practical work experience, thereby enabling not only enhancement of knowledge but also significant upskilling and improvement in professional productivity. The WILP portfolio spans multiple domains, including science, technology and engineering, management, as well as humanities and social sciences, and may lead to the award of degrees, diplomas, or certificates. The continued offering of these programmes was approved by the University Grants Commission in its 548th meeting held on September 9, 2020, based on the recommendation of the Empowered Expert Committee.

Registration and Fee Payment: Each semester begins with a mandatory registration process that specifies the prescribed course package for the student. The process is completed when the student submits a duly signed Registration Card along with the full payment of semester fees. Payment of the complete semester fee is required for every semester in which the student registers, irrespective of the number of courses included in the prescribed package.

Continuous Internal Evaluation: A continuous system of internal evaluation is followed, based on a relative grading methodology. Letter grades such as A, A-, B, B-, C, C-, D, and E are awarded for coursework, with corresponding grade points ranging from 10 to 2. For Dissertation or Project Work, non-letter grades such as EXCELLENT, GOOD, FAIR, and POOR are awarded. If a student does not provide adequate opportunity for evaluation, a report such as NC (Not Cleared) may be assigned.

Evaluation Scheme: Each course typically consists of three evaluation components conducted during the semester, as detailed in the course handout. The first component, EC-1, generally includes online assignments or quizzes with an indicative weightage of 30 percent. The second component, EC-2, is a mid-semester test carrying approximately 30 percent weightage. The third component, EC-3, is a comprehensive examination with an indicative weightage of 40 percent. All evaluation components are conducted by BITS Pilani faculty, and the exact weightage may vary from course to course.

Examination Centres: The EC-2 and EC-3 examinations are conducted only at designated examination centres across various cities. These centres include multiple locations within India, such as Chennai, Bangalore, Hyderabad, Mumbai, Delhi NCR, Pune, Kolkata, and others, along with international locations such as Dubai (BITS Campus), Abu Dhabi, Doha, Kuala Lumpur, Singapore, Riyadh, and Muscat. Due to operational considerations, certain centres may not be available in a given semester, and students may be required to appear at an alternate centre. Students opting for the Dubai examination centre are required to pay an additional examination fee.

Relative Grading Procedure: Final grades in each course are determined using a relative grading procedure. The total marks obtained by all students are arranged in descending order and analyzed in terms of average, highest, and lowest scores. The distribution is examined to identify clusters and gaps, which are then used to determine grade boundaries. The final grades are declared only after approval by the Examination Committee.

Minimum Academic Requirements: To successfully complete the programme, students must meet specified minimum academic standards. For postgraduate programmes, a minimum CGPA of 5.50 is required, while for undergraduate programmes, a minimum CGPA of 4.50 must be achieved. In addition, students must not have an E grade or an RRA report in any course. Students are not permitted to register for any other course alongside Dissertation or Project Work in the final semester. Those who fail to meet the minimum academic requirements are placed under the Academic Monitoring Board, which assigns an additional semester with a prescribed course package and closely monitors academic progress. Upon successful completion, students receive semester-wise grade sheets, a transcript, a provisional certificate, and eventually the final degree certificate.

Dissertation and Project Work: In the final semester, students are required to complete either a Dissertation or a Project Work component, depending on the programme structure. Registration for this component is permitted only after completion of all prescribed coursework and fulfilment of minimum academic requirements. If these requirements are not met, the student must re-register for certain courses in subsequent semesters to improve the CGPA before becoming eligible. As these programmes are work-integrated, any change in employment or mentor must be reported to and approved by the Dean. Failure to obtain such approval results in discontinuation from the programme and removal of the student from the institute rolls.
 
Library and E‑Resource Access: The Library provides access to a wide range of subscribed e-resources, including journals, databases, and e-books across multiple disciplines. All resources are accessible only through the authorized OpenAthens system, and access is strictly limited to content covered under the Institute’s subscription, which may vary by title, year, or collection. Users must access resources through the OpenAthens portal to ensure proper authentication, as direct access through publisher websites may not work. In case of access issues, users are required to contact the Library using their official Institute email ID and provide complete details such as the resource title, platform name, and relevant error messages or screenshots. The Library also assists with account-related queries, access issues, and platform-specific support, although there may be slight delays due to high query volumes.

ERP Access and Technical Issues: Students are required to access the ERP portal for registration, course selection, and academic tracking. If login errors such as “403 error” occur, users are advised to clear browser cache and cookies and use supported browsers such as Microsoft Edge or Google Chrome. Issues such as “Hold on Record” typically occur when fee payment is pending or not yet processed. Password-related issues for the Institute email must be resolved by contacting the designated email administrator.

Course Access and Academic Resources: Course materials, handouts, assignments, and related academic resources are made available on the eLearn portal and Microsoft Teams after registration is completed and before the start of classes. Course handouts include details such as syllabus, evaluation components, faculty information, and reference materials. Old question papers, course descriptions, and exam schedules can also be accessed through the eLearn portal. Quiz and assignment components are managed by the respective course faculty, who must be contacted for any issues related to submissions or deadlines.
 
Academic Performance and Progress Tracking: Students can monitor their academic progress through the ERP portal and performance sheets, which provide details of completed and pending courses. Grade sheets and CGPA details are available on the eLearn portal. Minimum CGPA requirements must be met for progression, especially for registration in the final semester. Students who do not meet academic requirements must continue with additional semesters to improve their performance.
